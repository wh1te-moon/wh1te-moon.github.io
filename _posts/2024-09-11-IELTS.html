  5. Yours sincerely:

    • Use this closing when you know the name of the person you are addressing. It is typically used in formal letters.
    • Example: If you start your letter with "Dear Mr. Smith," you would end it with "Yours sincerely."

  6. Yours faithfully:

    • This closing is appropriate when you do not know the name of the person you are addressing. It's often used in very formal correspondence.
    • Example: If you start your letter with "Dear Sir or Madam," you would end it with "Yours faithfully."

  7. Other common closings:

    • Best regards: This is less formal than "Yours sincerely" and is often used in business correspondence when you have a friendly relationship with the recipient.
    • Kind regards: Similar to "Best regards," this is also suitable for business correspondence but can convey a slightly warmer tone.
    • Warm regards: This is more personal and can be used when you have a closer relationship with the recipient.
    • Sincerely: This is a more general closing that can be used in both formal and informal letters.
